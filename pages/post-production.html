Documentation & Archiving Stage

I. Capturing the Performance: Photo and Video Archival

This stage focuses on comprehensively documenting the live performance. It involves capturing high-quality photographs and video recordings to preserve the theatrical act for future reference, academic analysis, and promotional purposes.

A. Performance Photography

The process of capturing still images of the live performance, focusing on key moments, performer interactions, scenic elements, and overall stage aesthetics. This section discusses photographic techniques suitable for live theater and the importance of visual storytelling through stills.

B. Live Performance Videography

Recording the entire theatrical act from multiple angles to create a comprehensive video archive. This section will detail camera setups, strategies for capturing live audio directly from the soundboard or with external microphones, and considerations for effectively documenting the ephemeral nature of a live performance.

A professional photographer capturing a pivotal moment during the live performance.

II. Post-Documentation Processing and Organization

After the live run, the captured documentation material is processed, edited, and organized for scholarly archival and presentation purposes.

A. Video Editing for Archival and Research Use

The process of editing the raw video footage to create both a full archival recording and shorter excerpts or highlights for research and presentation. This involves syncing captured audio, color correction (for video), and assembling the best takes to accurately represent the live theatrical experience.

B. Audio Post-Processing for Documentation

Refining the recorded audio from the live performance or the documentation video to ensure optimal clarity, balance, and effectiveness for archival purposes. This may include noise reduction, level adjustments, and mastering for consistent quality.

C. Digital Asset Management and Archiving

Organizing all digital assets (performance photos, video recordings, audio files, and all production-related documents) into a structured and easily accessible archive. This includes meticulous metadata tagging, file naming conventions, and strategies for long-term digital preservation.

An editor working on the video documentation of the performance for archival and research.

Organizing and cataloging digital assets for secure, long-term archival storage.

This section will also include samples of edited performance footage, high-resolution performance photos, and a breakdown of the archival structure.

III. Final Presentation and Reflection

The culmination of the entire project, encompassing the successful presentation of the live performance and the finalization of its comprehensive documentary. This stage also includes critical reflection and analysis of the entire production process.

A. Thesis Documentation Finalization

The compilation and finalization of all documentation, including written analysis, visual assets, audio recordings, and video materials, for submission as the complete thesis.
